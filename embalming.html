Embalming

Embalming is the process of preserving a dead body by injecting it with Arsenic. This prevents the process of decay from happening and was originally used by anatomists to dissect bodies of animals. When the Civil War happened, there was a want for fallen soldiers to return home. Embalming became a popular method after an Army Medical Corps colonel became the first Union officer to be killed, on May 24, 1861. Embalmers were able to make profits of $100 per corpse.
