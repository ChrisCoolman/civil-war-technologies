Ironclad Ships

Ironclad ships are warships made of iron instead of wood, making them more durable. Originally used in Europe, Captain James Buchanan Eads used them in the Civil War to make armoured gunboats for fights in the mississippi river. These boats were successful in capturing the Confederate's Fort Henry.

Clash of the Ironclads

On March 9, 1862, the USS Monitor and CSS Virginia met on the Chesapeake bay. The fight between them is the first time these types of ships have fought another. The battle ended in a draw as they shot each other for hours which dealt no damage, and both sides gave up.
