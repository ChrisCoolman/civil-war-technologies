The Minié Ball

The Minié Ball is a small yet deadly bullet used in the Civil War. It was used due to its accuraacy and speed. It was created by Claude-Etienne Minié in 1849 who had the idea of a bullet that expanded upon firing. The grooves on the side of the Minié Ball helped speed it up and make it spin when it was fired from a repeating rifle. It had a range of 200-250 yards.
